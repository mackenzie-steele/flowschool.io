The Formula

Three parts. One clear cue.

Most unclear cues fail in one of three places: no action word, a vague body part, or no destination. This formula fixes all three at once.

01 — Verb Sweep
+
02 — Your Body Part your arms
+
03 — Direction up and over your head
= Clear Cue
Examples
Sweep/ your arms/ up and over your head
Lift/ your right knee/ up level with your hip
Step/ your left foot/ to the top of the mat between your hands
Push/ your heels/ to the back of the room
Hug/ your low ribs/ in

Why it works

The verb gives the student a job. The body part tells them what to move. The direction tells them where to take it. Together, they create a cue that is specific enough to follow, short enough to land during movement, and kind enough that it never sounds like a correction.
