Elements of Flow Lab

Learn what makes a flow feel complete, natural, and alive.

A Bonnie class is not just a sequence of poses. It is a complete movement experience — mobility, stability, strength, conditioning, flow, breath, flexibility, and rest woven together with intention. This lab helps you explore the ingredients of Flow School through movement planes, body positions, gait, and the simple reminder to move, breathe, and rest.

A Peak Flow does not mean the hardest flow. A Peak Flow means it includes all body positions and all movement planes.

Peak Flow Checklist

Body Position Check

As you build your Peak Flow, check off each body position your flow includes. The examples under each position are just illustrations — any pose that fits the pattern counts. One pose can cover multiple positions at once — Warrior 3, for example, is single leg, straight leg, and square hip simultaneously. A complete Peak Flow covers all 13.

Natural Rhythm

Gait Check

Gait is the natural rhythm of walking — opposite arms and legs move together. When a flow uses alternation, it often feels more organic, like the body already knows where to go next. Tap a pair to explore.

Move — Breathe — Rest
