Breath Pace for Beginners

When you move through flow continuously, not teaching it, just moving, it’s often much faster than when you cue it. Before you practice cueing a flow with this, move through it with the timer to feel the natural rhythm of your breath in continuous motion. This is not meant to rush you, but guide you to even breathing and give awareness to timing.

4 seconds is a common breath pace while in motion. When you practice at this pace, name an inhale/exhale, one short cue instruction, and make a breath noise (either inhale or exhale). Experiment with shorter and longer breaths. It’s helpful to voice record yourself cueing and then practice back to your cues. When you listen back, only move and breathe when instructed to get the authentic experience.

This tool is here to help you practice timing until it becomes something you can trust in your body.

Why this matters

Breath pace is where the yoga dance of sequencing meets the rhythm of the body. When you design flow to move like a wave, to expand and contract, to work with the human gait, it mimics the breath. When you combine the body and the breath, especially when the sequencing experience is equal parts challenge and success, you can enter a flow state. Flow state is highly satisfying and euphoric. It’s when the world melts away and we’re right here, right now.
